Part II — Why Problems Persist

Four Root Causes

Surface problems are symptoms. These four causes explain why failures keep recurring despite significant investment.

01
Technology Built Top-Down
CCTNS, TG Cops — all designed for command visibility. The constable generating data gets more work with no benefit. When technology gives nothing to the person using it, adoption fails universally.
📊 Result: 60% utilization despite years of investment — DGP CV Anand, June 2026
02
Data Entered Twice For No Reason
Officers write on paper (they must — it's the legal original), then re-type every field into CCTNS. Doubles time, introduces court-level transcription errors, creates resentment toward the system.
🤖 Maharashtra MahaCrimeOS: 1 case/month → 7–8 cases/month after removing this friction
03
Cybercrime Reported After It Happened
The entire system is reactive. By the time investigation begins, the fraudster has moved. No pre-crime intelligence exists. No way to detect active fraud while the attack infrastructure is live.
⏱️ The 72-hour cold trail: every traceable element is gone before investigation starts
04
Identity Theft Starts at the Print Shop
Commercial printers store every document ever printed on internal hard drives. Aadhaar cards, bank statements, marksheets — all cached. When printers are sold secondhand, that data is exposed. This enables identity theft, KYC fraud, and SIM swap before any crime reaches a police station.
🖨️ No existing mechanism addresses this silent source of identity compromise in Hyderabad

Part V — Our Proposals

Four Solutions, One Direction

Each proposal addresses documented root causes independently. Together they form a complete loop — before, during, and after crime reaches the station.

Proposal 01 · Cybercrime
Pre-Crime Fraud
Intelligence System
A citizen-facing tool that analyzes suspicious links BEFORE users click them — and automatically reports confirmed fraud to a Cyberabad Police dashboard in real time. Police see attacks while they're happening, not 3 days after the damage is done.
💡 The Key Insight
Existing tools ask: "Is this technically malicious?"
That's the wrong question. Most Indian cyberfraud has no malware — it just manipulates a human who is scared, rushed, or confused.

The right question is: "Should I trust this right now?" — in Telugu, for a first-time smartphone user in Hyderabad.
WHAT IT SOLVES
✓ 5% arrest rate — detecting fraud while perpetrator is still active
✓ The 72-hour cold trail problem
✓ No proactive citizen safety tool in Telugu
✓ No aggregated fraud intelligence for police

10+ reports of same link = police alert

Proposal 04 · Hardware · Identity Prevention
Print Shop Document
Security Box
A small plug-in hardware device that sits between the shopkeeper's computer and the printer. It automatically erases sensitive documents from the printer's internal memory after each print job — without requiring the shopkeeper to do anything. Plug in. Done.
💡 The Silent Problem No One Is Talking About
When a citizen sends their Aadhaar card, bank statement, property paper, or marksheet to a print shop — that document gets stored on the printer's internal hard drive. The shopkeeper doesn't know. The citizen doesn't know.

When the printer is sold secondhand — which happens constantly — someone with basic technical knowledge can pull thousands of documents off that drive. That is where your citizens' identities are leaking.
Two types of printers — very different risk
🖨️
Low Risk
Basic consumer printers use RAM buffers that clear when the print job finishes or on restart.
Epson, Canon, standard HP
🖨️
HIGH RISK ⚠️
Office/commercial printers have internal hard drives that store every document ever printed. These are the printers in Hyderabad print shops.
Sharp, Xerox, Ricoh, Konica Minolta
DOWNSTREAM CRIMES THIS PREVENTS
🪪 Identity Theft
Stolen documents used to impersonate citizens at banks, courts, and government offices
📋 KYC Fraud
Fake KYC submissions using real citizen Aadhaar and PAN data from printer drives
💳 Loan Fraud
Loans opened in victim's name using their stolen Aadhaar, bank details, and address proof
📱 SIM Swap Fraud
New SIM cards obtained using stolen identity documents — enables full account takeover
Hardware specifications
Core Hardware
Raspberry Pi (or equivalent single-board computer)
Internet Required
None — fully offline operation
Shopkeeper Action
Plug in. Nothing else required.
Authentication
PIN or fingerprint module (optional)
Printer Protocol
PJL, SNMP, vendor-specific admin API
How it works — full pipeline
1
Shopkeeper Installs the Box
The device sits between the shopkeeper's computer and the commercial printer. One cable. No configuration. No internet needed.
2
Citizen Sends Document to Print
Normal process — citizen sends Aadhaar, bank statement, property paper. Shopkeeper prints as usual. Nothing changes for the citizen.
3
Box Detects Print Job Completion
Hardware monitors printer communication. As soon as the job finishes, it triggers the erase protocol automatically.
4
Auto-Erase Sent to Printer
Firmware sends the correct erase command for that printer model (PJL overwrite, SNMP, or vendor admin API). Printer internal memory cleared.
5
Confirmation LED / Log
Green LED on the box confirms successful erase. Local log entry stored (anonymized — no document content, just timestamp + printer model + success/fail).
6
Shopkeeper Sees Nothing Change
The box operates silently. The shopkeeper's workflow is identical. The citizen's document is protected without either party doing anything differently.
🧪 Pilot approach: Start with 5–10 print shops near police stations, courts, and government offices — highest density of sensitive document printing. Anonymized data on erase success rates and shopkeeper adoption generates the evidence base for wider deployment.
