Part II — Why Problems Persist

Four Root Causes

Surface problems are symptoms. Understanding why failures keep recurring despite significant investment is what separates a useful proposal from a sales pitch.

01
Technology Built Top-Down
Every major system — CCTNS, TG Cops — was designed for command-level visibility. Senior officers get dashboards. The constable generating the data gets more work with no personal benefit. When technology gives nothing to the person asked to use it, adoption fails.
📊 Result: 60% utilization despite years of investment — DGP CV Anand, June 2026
02
Data Entered Twice For No Reason
Officers write on paper (they must — it's the legal original), then re-type every field into CCTNS. This doubles time, introduces court-level transcription errors, and creates resentment. The paper exists. The information is written. Asking for it again is the problem.
🤖 Maharashtra proof: MahaCrimeOS AI — 1 case/month → 7–8 cases/month. Expanding to 1,100 stations.
03
Cybercrime Reported After It Happened
The entire system is reactive. Crime occurs. Victim discovers it hours later. They report it. Investigation begins. By then the fraudster has changed numbers, closed accounts, moved servers. No pre-crime intelligence exists. No way to detect active fraud while infrastructure is live.
⏱️ The 72-hour problem: every traceable element is gone before investigation begins.
04
No Accountability Layer
When a document enters the system incorrectly, there is no way to trace: who entered it, when, whether it matches the original, or whether it was altered. Cases auto-close with no action. Citizens receive no communication. The system is invisible to those it serves.
🔍 CAG Audit 2025: 1,631 breaches in 405 stations — backend tampering cited as cause.

No ticket number. No assigned officer name. No status update. Citizen must physically revisit or call to know anything.
✅ Fix: real-time case tracker with SMS updates at each stage

Wrong challan — no dispute path
eChallan portal exists but disputing requires a physical visit. No photo upload, no hearing scheduler, no online appeal.
✅ Fix: dispute form with photo upload, auto hearing date, SMS confirmation

No way to escalate or give feedback
If an officer is unresponsive, there's no structured channel to rate the experience or escalate.
✅ Fix: post-case rating system routed to SI, with anonymous escalation option
🤖
No guided help for first-timers
Citizens don't know which form to file, what documents to carry, or which dept to contact for cybercrime vs theft.
✅ Fix: chatbot that asks 3 questions and routes citizen to the right service

Part V — Our Proposals

Three Solutions, One Direction

Each proposal addresses documented root causes. They work independently but become more powerful in combination. None of them replace existing systems.

Proposal 01 · Cybercrime
Pre-Crime Fraud
Intelligence System
A citizen-facing tool that analyzes suspicious links BEFORE users click them — and automatically reports confirmed fraud patterns to a police dashboard in real time. Police see attacks while they're happening, not 3 days after the damage is done.
💡 The Real Question Citizens Need Answered
Not: "Is this website dangerous?"
But: "Should I trust this right now?"

No existing tool answers the second question — especially not in Telugu or Hindi, especially not for a first-time smartphone user in Hyderabad.
WHAT IT SOLVES
✓ 5% arrest rate — detecting fraud while perpetrator is still active
✓ The 72-hour evidence cold trail problem
✓ No proactive citizen safety tool in Telugu
✓ No aggregated fraud intelligence for police
